\textpr[20mm]{%
  \textbf{The Man and the Elevator}\footnotemark
  \\[1ex]
  \zcomi{Order}{排順序} the following statements.\\
  \zcomi{Solve}{解答} the riddle.
  }{../common/graphics/misc/elevatorman4955924.jpg}%
\footnotetext{\url{http://brainden.com/logic-puzzles.htm}}
\\
\begin{tabular}{|>{\scriptsize}rcp{\tw-30mm}|}
  \hline
  \cntAo & \bop [10mm]{1/2/$\cdots$/6}{3} & Every evening he returns and gets on the same elevator.
  \cntAn & \bop [10mm]{1/2/$\cdots$/6}{5} & Otherwise, he only rides the elevator to the seventh floor, and then takes the stairs to the tenth floor.
  \cntAn & \bop [10mm]{1/2/$\cdots$/6}{2} & Every morning he leaves his apartment, takes the elevator to the first floor and goes to work.
  \cntAn & \bop [10mm]{1/2/$\cdots$/6}{4} & If other people are on the elevator or if it is a rainy day, he rides the elevator directly to the tenth floor.
  \cntAn & \bop [10mm]{1/2/$\cdots$/6}{1} & A man lives in a tenth floor apartment.
  \cntAn & \bops[10mm]{1/2/$\cdots$/6}{6} & Why?
  \\\hline
\end{tabular}

\zcomi{Solution}{解答}/\zcomi{Answer}{答案}:
The man is \bsoAo[30mm]{short} and therefore can only \bsoA[30mm]{reach} the \bsoA[30mm]{button} for the seventh floor.
%\bsoA[30mm]{button} for the \bsoA[30mm]{seventh} \bsoA[30mm]{floor}.
%If there are \bsoA[30mm]{other} \bsoA[30mm]{people}, he can ask them to \bsoA[30mm]{push} the button for the tenth floor.
If there are other people, he can \bsoA[30mm]{ask} them to \bsoA[30mm]{push} the \bsoA[30mm]{button} for the tenth floor.
%\bsoA[30mm]{button} for the \bsoA[30mm]{tenth} \bsoA[30mm]{floor}.
If it is {raining}, he has an \bsoA[30mm]{umbrella} and can use it to \bsoA[30mm]{push} the \bsoA[30mm]{button} for the tenth floor.

\text{The Lost Colony}

In 1587, ninety men, seventeen women, and nine children left England on a ship
to start a new \zcomi{colony}{殖民地} in the \zcomi{Americas}{美洲}.
%The captain of the ship was Simon Fernandes from \zcomi{Portugal}{葡萄牙}.
The captain was supposed to take the \zcomi{colonists}{殖民地開拓者} to Chesapeake Bay.
But instead he took them to a small island called \emph{Roanoke} off the coast of North Carolina.
He said it was too late in the year to take them any farther.
%Fernandes stayed about a month, and then left.

The 116 people set up a small colony on Roanoke.
John White was the colony's \zcomi{governor}{州長}.
His daughter Eleanor was also in the colony.
She gave birth to a baby girl shortly after arriving in Roanoke,
perhaps the first white baby to ever be born in the Americas.

After his granddaughter was born, John White left with the captain to return to England for more \zcomi{supplies}{用品}.
He told them before he left, that if they had any trouble, they should \zcomi{carve}{刻}
a \zcomi{cross}{十字形} on a tree.

But there was \zcomi{war}{戰爭} between England and Spain.
And John White did not manage to return until three years later.
But when he did finally return, no one was there.
All 116 people---men, women, and children---were gone.
They were never seen by another white person again.

Things seemed to be fairly in order in Roanoke.
There were no dead bodies. There were no bones. There was no sign of fighting.
And no carving of a cross was found.
But the letters ``CROATANS" had been carved on a post and the letters ``CRO" had been carved on a tree.
Did that mean they went to live on nearby Croatan Island?

But years later in 1607, after the new colony of Jamestown was started, a colonist named George Percy
reported seeing an \zcomi{indian}{印第安人} with yellow hair and fairly white skin. %\citerpg{aron2005}{181}{1851098992}
A man named John Smith wrote that an indian chief told him about some men who were clothed like Smith
and who lived in houses with stone walls.
Another story said that the Roanoke colonist lived in Chesapeake Bay for about twenty years with the Chesapeake Indians;
but then another indian named Powhatan went to war with the Chesapeake Indians
and killed both the Chesapeake Indians and the Roanoke colonists.

Where did the colonists go and what happened to them?
They were never found.
And to this day, no one knows for sure what happened to them.

\banner{Living in a Cave}{住在山洞裡}
%=======================================
\includegraphics*[height=50mm]{../common/graphics/people/maurizio_montalbin_dtelegraph.jpg}\cittr{montalbini2009}
\includegraphics*[height=50mm]{../common/graphics/photos/frasassi.jpg}\footnote{\url{http://www.frasassi.com}}
%\rgraphic{100mm}{100mm}{../common/graphics/people/maurizio_montalbin_wsj.jpg}\cittr{montalbini2009}

% Maurizio Montalbini (1953 September 4 --- 2009 September 19

Maurizio Montalbini was a \zcomi{sociologist}{社會學家}.
He worked with \zcomi{drug addicts}{吸毒者}.
But later he tried something else --- living underground in caves.

On December 14, 1986 when Maurizio was 33 years old, he entered the Frasassi Caves in Italy.
He lived there alone underground with no sunlight for months.
While there he ate powdered food and pills similar to those used by \zcomi{astronauts}{太空人} in space.
%Scientists on the surface used \zcomi{instruments}{儀器} to \zcomi{monitor}{監控} him.
When he finally came out, he thought 79 days had passed.
He was wrong.
It was now July 12, 1987 --- he had been underground for 210 days.
%He found that time seemed to pass more quickly while underground.

This was not the last time he stayed in a cave.
On December 6, 1992 when Maurizio was 39 years old,
he entered a cave in Pesaro and didn't come back out until December 5, 1993.
Again he found that time seemed to pass more quickly while underground ---
he thought it was only June 6 when he came out.

In 1997 when Maurizio was 44 years old,
he again entered a cave and stayed for 166 days to come out in April 1998.
This time someone asked him if he preferred staying in caves.
Maurizio replied,
``Are you trying to be funny? I'm not going back in there.
I need the sun. I used to dream about the dawn.
It's an experience I would not repeat."\cittr{montalbini2009}

But he was wrong.
In October 2006 when Maurizio was 53 years old,
he entered a cave called the \emph{Grotta Fredda} (``Cold Cave"), located about 80 meters underground in Italy.
This time he also brought with him 4 kilograms of honey, 2 kilograms of walnuts, 1.5 kilograms of chocolate,
and 85 books.
Most of the time he was in the dark. But he did allow himself to read a few minutes each day using a gas light.
He planned to stay there for three years.
But he only ended up staying for 235 days.
Maurizaio said,
``This is the last experiment I'm going to do, I'm getting too old for this."\cittr{bbc_montalbini2006}

And indeed it really was his last.
On September 19, 2009, at age 56, Maurizio died of a \zcomi{stroke}{中風}.
Maurizio was married, but had no children.
The stroke is not known to have been caused by his time living underground.

After his 1987 cave experience, Maurizio said,
``One cannot fight \zcomi{solitude}{孤獨}, one must make a friend of it.
I succeeded in doing this. I carried everything inside me for seven months ---
\zcomi{affections}{情愛}, \zcomi{convictions}{說服力}, \zcomi{ideals}{理想}."%
\cittr{montalbini2009}

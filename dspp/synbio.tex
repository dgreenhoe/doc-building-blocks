  \item What might a DNA machine be able to do?
    \begin{enumerate}
      \item Detect danger (viruses or poisonous gases).
      \item Find and destroy cancer \hiv{cell}s.
      \item Produce fuel (biofuel).
      \item DNA supercomputers.
      \item DNA \hiv{hard drive}s: \url{http://www.popsci.com/dna-data-storage-can-last-2000-years}\\
            A normal \hiv{hard drive} can store data for maybe 50 years.\\
            A DNA hard drive could store data for maybe 2000 years.\\
            \begin{tabular}{lll}
              data writing & (need a \hiv{DNA synthesizer}): & very expensive now\\
              data reading & (need a \hiv{DNA sequencer}):   & getting cheaper and cheaper\\
              data copying &                                & very easy
            \end{tabular}
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\headbox{How do I make a genetic machine?}{}
%=======================================
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \hiv{Design} the DNA sequence: \begin{tabular}{c}\includegraphics[height=25mm]{../common/english/dna/dna_hardhat_af4969017.jpg}\end{tabular}\citetbl{image: \citeWc{afact}{af4969017}}
    \begin{enumerate}
      \item There is a free tool for this that you can download called ``\hie{Gene Designer 2.0}:
        \\\url{https://www.dna20.com/resources/genedesigner}
        \\\begin{tabular}{*{3}{>{\scs}c}}
           \includegraphics[height=15mm]{../common/english/dna/dna20_genedesigner20.jpg}

        \end{tabular}\citetbl{images: \url{https://www.dna20.com/resources/genedesigner}}

      \item You can download DNA parts for free from 
        \begin{enumerate}
          \item the \hie{Registry of Standard Biological Parts} (over 20,000 parts!): 
              \\\url{http://parts.igem.org/}
          \item the \hie{BioBricks Foundation}: \url{https://biobricks.org/}
        \end{enumerate}

      \item Here are some examples:
        \begin{enumerate}
          \item proteins that give off light (fluorescent proteins): 
              \\\url{https://biobricks.org/bpa/contributions/62/}
          \item logic gates (AND, OR, NAND, XOR, etc): \url{https://biobricks.org/bpa/contributions/57/}  
        \end{enumerate}
    \end{enumerate}

  \item \hiv{Synthesize} the DNA sequence into a DNA amino acid chain:
    \begin{enumerate}
      \item There are companies that will do this for you.  %https://www.researchgate.net/post/Which_company_for_gene_synthesis
        \begin{enumerate}
          \item \hie{Genescript}: 3000bp, US\$690/NT\$21000, 15 days, 
              \\\url{http://www.genscript.com/gene_synthesis.html}
          \item \hie{Biomatik}: 3000bp, US\$840/NT\$25000, 3 weeks, 
                \url{http://www.biomatik.com/services/gene-synthesis-gene-synthesis/gene-synthesis.html} 
          \item \hie{Geneart}: 3000bp, 12 days, 
                \url{https://www.thermofisher.com/tw/en/home/life-science/cloning/gene-synthesis/geneart-gene-synthesis.html}
          \item \hie{Genewiz}: 3000bp, 11--15 days, 
              \\\url{http://www.genewiz.com/public/gene-synthesis.aspx}
          \item \hie{DNA2.0}:  3000bp, 12 days, 
              \\\url{https://www.dna20.com/services/gene-synthesis}
        \end{enumerate}

      \item You can buy a \hie{DNA synthesizer} (``DNA printer") and do it yourself\ldots\\
